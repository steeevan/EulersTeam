Information on various parts of the Solar System

ASTEROIDS: Formed in the warmer inner solar system between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter. Today’s asteroids are the bits and pieces left over from the initial agglomeration of the inner planets that include Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars. Sizes range from hundreds of kilometers wide to less than a kilometer.

COMETS: Composed mostly of water ice with embedded dust particles. The giant outer planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune) formed from an agglomeration of billions of comets and the left over bits and pieces from this formation process comets. Small, irregularly shaped, icy surface embedded from dust, grit, and particles from space.

METEOROIDS: Smaller fragments of asteroids and dust from comets. Meteoroids are small and rocky. Most smaller than a grain of sand Burns up in Earth’s atmosphere creating shooting stars. Meteoroids on the ground are meteorites, most are microscopic. Cause serious damage in proportion to size

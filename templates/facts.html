Information on various parts of the Solar System

ASTEROIDS: Formed in the warmer inner solar system between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter. Today’s asteroids are the bits and pieces left over from the initial agglomeration of the inner planets that include Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars. Sizes range from hundreds of kilometers wide to less than a kilometer.

COMETS: Composed mostly of water ice with embedded dust particles. The giant outer planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune) formed from an agglomeration of billions of comets and the left over bits and pieces from this formation process comets. Small, irregularly shaped, icy surface embedded from dust, grit, and particles from space.

METEOROIDS: Smaller fragments of asteroids and dust from comets. Meteoroids are small and rocky. Most smaller than a grain of sand Burns up in Earth’s atmosphere creating shooting stars. Meteoroids on the ground are meteorites, most are microscopic. Cause serious damage in proportion to size

Mercury: First planet from the Sun, and smallest in the solar system. One of the terrestrial planets. Has the most eccentric orbit in the solar system. Named after the Roman god of messengers.

Venus: Second planet from the Sun, and sixth largest. A terrestrial planet, and the hottest planet in the solar system Named after the Roman love goddess. Surface temperature hot enough to melt lead.

Earth: The planet we live on. Third planet from the Sun, and terrestrial planet. Only known planet to support life. Is the fifth largest planet. One moon, our Earth. In the area known as the "goldilocks" zone.

Mars: Mars is the fourth Planet from the Sun. Named for Roman god of war Its red because of oxidized iron in its soil Martian days are called sols. It has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos

Jupiter: Largest and oldest planet in the solar system, and the Oldest planet in the solar system. Named after the king of Roman gods Has 95 moons total, and Four largest moons discovered by Galileo

Saturn: Sixth planet from the Sun, and second largest in the solar system. One of the gas giant planets. Named for Roman god of agriculture & wealth. Has rings, and 146 moons in orbit.

Uranus: Seventh planet from the Sun, and appears to spin sideways. Named for the Greek of the sky. Has 28 known moons. Ice giant, and planet is mostly swirling fluids

Neptune: Farthest planet from the Sun. First planet located by mathematical calculations. 16 known moons, ice giant, and the windiest planet.
